I am a UX Designer with a background in Visual Arts and Communications. I am both creative and analytical. I graduated from the University of Georgia with a Bachelor of Arts degree in History, a Studio Art Minor, and a Museum Studies Certificate in December of 2019. From my degree, I gained research, writing, and presentation skills. I specialized in public history with an emphasis on diversity and technology. While in school, I led a large student organization for two years as president. As a communications intern, I wrote press releases and edited other marketing materials in the communications department at the Georgia Museum of Art. My training as a historian makes me an excellent researcher, writer, and presenter. I graduated from the Georgia Tech UX/UI boot camp in September of 2020. I worked alongside a team to design two original mobile applications and a responsive web design website. Some transferable skills to UX from previous roles include time management, editing, and collaboration.
